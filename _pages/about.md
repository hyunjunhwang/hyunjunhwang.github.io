I am a Ph.D. candidate in Transportation Engineering at the University of Texas at Austin, advised by Professor Chandra R. Bhat, and I expect to graduate in December 2026. I am a transportation researcher specializing in the econometric analysis of individual choice behavior. My work develops and applies discrete choice and spatial econometric models across two substantive streams: *travel behavior* — how people decide when, how, and with whom to travel, with a focus on active modes such as walking — and *transportation policy perception* — how the public forms attitudes toward measures such as congestion pricing and road-usage charges. Bridging the two is a methodological agenda: building choice models that capture the spatial, multidimensional, and heterogeneous nature of travel-related decisions.

Research Interests
======
- Travel behavior, with an emphasis on active travel (walking and bicycling)
- Public perception and acceptance of transportation policy
- Discrete choice and spatial econometric modeling

I am on the academic job market for faculty positions beginning in 2027.

Research Themes
======
 
**Active Travel Behavior**
 
I study walking and other active modes as inherently multidimensional behaviors — spanning the frequency, duration, and social context of travel — shaped jointly by individual, life-course, and environmental factors. This work develops joint and multivariate choice models that capture how these dimensions respond together to behavioral and environmental influences.
 
- [Multivariate Analysis of Frequency, Duration and Companionship in Walking Behaviors Among Adults Over 50](/publication/2025-walking-companionship) (*Journal of Transport & Health*, 2025)
- [Walking Patterns in Older Adults: Modeling the Interplay of Frequency, Place, and Purpose](/publication/2025-walking-patterns) (*Journal of Transport & Health*, 2025)
- [An Analysis of Walking Frequency Before and After the Pandemic](/publication/2023-walking-frequency-pandemic) (Technical paper)
**Transportation Policy Perception**
 
I examine how the public forms attitudes toward transportation policy — including congestion pricing and road-usage charges — and what shapes support for or opposition to these measures. This work applies discrete choice and perception models to understand the behavioral basis of policy acceptance.
 
- [A Tale of Two Perspectives: The Mileage-based User Fee Conundrum](/publication/2026-mbuf) (*Transportation Research Part A*, in press)
- [Understanding Public Perceptions of Congestion Pricing — A U.S. Perspective](/publication/2026-congestion-pricing) (*Transport Policy*, under review)
- [Trapped by Transport: Exploring the Everyday Impact of Mobility Challenges and Transportation Insecurities in the United States](/publication/2026-transport-insecurity) (*Travel Behaviour and Society*, under review)
**Spatial Modeling Advances**
 
I develop spatial econometric methods tailored to travel behavior — featuring localized spillover effects whose spatial reach is estimated from the data, a separate treatment of observed and unobserved spatial dependence, and flexible error distributions that accommodate behavioral asymmetry.
 
- [Spatial Ordered Probit Model with Heteroscedastic Skewed Errors and Exogenous Spillovers](/publication/2026-spatial-ordered-probit) (*Transportation Research Part B*, under review)
